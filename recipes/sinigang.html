Filipino Sinigang

Prep: 20 mins Cook: 1 hour Serves: 6 4.7 (142 reviews)

Sinigang is a beloved Filipino soup known for its sour and savory flavors. This comforting dish features tender pork and a variety of vegetables in a tamarind-based broth, creating a perfect balance of tastes and textures.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork belly, cut into chunks
  • 2 packs sinigang mix
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 eggplants, sliced
  • 1 bundle string beans
  • 2 cups kangkong leaves
  • 3 pieces green chili
  • 8 cups water
  • Fish sauce to taste

Nutrition Facts

Calories 420
Protein 32g
Carbs 12g
Fat 28g

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, bring water to a boil and add pork. Simmer for 30 minutes or until tender.

  2. Add onions and tomatoes. Continue to simmer for 5-8 minutes.

  3. Add sinigang mix and stir until well combined.

  4. Add eggplant and string beans. Cook for 5 minutes.

  5. Add green chili peppers and kangkong leaves.

  6. Season with fish sauce to taste.

  7. Simmer for 2-3 more minutes. Serve hot with rice.

Recipe Notes

  • You can use fresh tamarind instead of sinigang mix for a more authentic taste.
  • Feel free to adjust the sourness by adding more or less sinigang mix.
  • Other vegetables like radish or okra can be added.
  • You can substitute pork with fish, shrimp, or beef.
