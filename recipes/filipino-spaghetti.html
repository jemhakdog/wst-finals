Filipino Style Spaghetti

Prep: 10 mins Cook: 45 mins Serves: 6-8 5.0 (1 review)

  • Email

Filipino Spaghetti is a unique twist on the classic Italian dish, featuring a sweet-style sauce and the addition of sliced hotdogs. This beloved comfort food is a staple at Filipino celebrations and gatherings, known for its distinctively sweet taste that sets it apart from its Italian counterpart.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb. spaghetti
  • 1 lb. ground pork
  • 1 Knorr Pork Cube
  • 3 tablespoons liver spread
  • 12 hotdogs, sliced
  • 35 ounces Filipino Style Spaghetti Sauce
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 onion, minced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • Shredded cheddar cheese for topping

Nutrition Facts

Calories 622
Protein 27g
Carbs 64g
Fat 29g

Instructions

  1. Cook the spaghetti noodles (al dente) according to package instructions. Once cooked, drain the water and transfer the spaghetti to a bowl. Cover and set aside.

  2. Heat the oil in a pan. Sauté the minced onion and garlic until the onions soften.

  3. Add the ground pork and cook until the color turns light brown.

  4. Add the sliced hotdogs and continue sautéing for 30 seconds.

  5. Add the flour and continue cooking for 1 minute.

  6. Pour in the Filipino Style Spaghetti sauce and water. Stir continuously until the sauce smoothens. Cover the pan and let it boil.

  7. Add the Knorr Pork Cube and cook for 15 to 18 minutes or until the sauce thickens.

  8. Add the liver spread and season with salt and ground black pepper as needed.

  9. Add the cooked spaghetti to the pan and toss until it is completely coated with the sauce.

  10. Top with shredded cheddar cheese before serving.

Recipe Notes

  • For best results, cook the pasta al dente as it will continue to cook slightly when mixed with the hot sauce.
  • Filipino Style Spaghetti Sauce can be found in Asian grocery stores, or you can make your own using tomato sauce and banana ketchup.
  • The liver spread adds richness to the sauce and is a key ingredient in Filipino-style spaghetti.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.
